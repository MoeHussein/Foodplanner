Chicken recipes

Four recipes, improved and ready to cook.

01

Mix sauce and slurry separately

Combine soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, and pepper in a bowl. In a separate cup, dissolve cornstarch in cold water. Keep them apart until step 4.

02

Sear the chicken dry

Pat every piece completely dry. Heat oil over medium-high until shimmering. Sear in batches without crowding, 3–4 min, turning once, until golden and just cooked through. Remove and set aside.

03

Reduce the sauce in the same pan

Lower to medium. Pour the full sauce into the pan — the browned bits from the chicken dissolve straight into it. Simmer 2–3 min until the garlic smells cooked and the honey begins to thicken.

04

Thicken, then coat

Stir the slurry and pour into the simmering sauce. It thickens in under 60 seconds. Once glossy and coating a spoon, return the chicken, toss to coat, and pull off the heat immediately.

05

Garnish and serve

Scatter sesame seeds and green onion. Serve over rice while the glaze is still fluid.

Optional

A few drops of toasted sesame oil off the heat adds depth. Chili flakes in the sauce balance the honey's sweetness.

01

Build the marinade

Whisk yogurt, mustard, olive oil, garlic, half the lemon juice (7ml), salt, pepper, paprika, coriander, and cardamom until smooth. Don't skip the cardamom — it's what makes this dish.

02

Marinate — minimum 30 minutes

Coat the chicken completely, cover, and refrigerate. The yogurt's lactic acid tenderizes the meat — give it time. You'll taste the difference at 60 min versus 0.

03

Cook in batches, medium-high

No extra oil needed — the marinade has fat. Hot dry pan, single layer, undisturbed 3–4 min. The yogurt solids form a golden-brown char on the outside — that's exactly what you want. Flip and repeat. Never crowd the pan.

04

Rest 2 minutes off the heat

Pull the chicken off and let it sit. Carryover heat finishes the center without drying the edges.

05

Finish with lemon and taste for salt

Squeeze the remaining 7ml lemon juice over the rested chicken. Taste — yogurt mutes salinity as it cooks, so you'll almost always need a pinch more here.

Optional

A pinch of turmeric in the marinade adds color with no flavor impact. A yogurt and cucumber sauce on the side adds a cool contrast.

01

Season and sear the chicken

Toss chicken with olive oil, half the curry powder (7g), paprika, salt, and pepper. Heat 14g butter over medium-high and sear until golden. Doesn't need to be fully cooked yet. Remove and set aside.

02

Brown mushrooms until bone dry

Same pan, no extra fat. Cook undisturbed 2–3 min, then stir. Keep going until all water has evaporated and the edges are golden. Wet mushrooms will break the cream sauce. Remove and set aside.

03

Deglaze the pan

Pour a splash of broth (~50ml) into the hot pan and scrape up all browned bits with a wooden spoon. Don't skip this — those bits are concentrated flavor.

04

Bloom spices, then build the base

Add remaining butter (14g). Once melted, add remaining curry powder (7g) and stir 30 seconds until fragrant. Add garlic (30 sec), then onion (5 min) until soft and translucent.

05

Make the roux

Sprinkle flour over the onions. Stir constantly for 1 full minute until the raw flour smell disappears and the color shifts slightly golden.

06

Add broth, then cream

Pour in remaining broth gradually, stirring constantly until smooth. Add cream. Simmer gently on medium-low — a hard boil will split the sauce.

07

Combine, finish, taste for salt

Return chicken and mushrooms to the pan. Simmer on low 3–5 min until the sauce is thick and glossy. Taste before serving — cream mutes salt as it reduces.

Optional

Frozen peas at step 7 add color and sweetness. A squeeze of lemon at the end cuts through the richness of the cream.

01

Mix the spice blend

Combine paprika, cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, oregano, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Set aside — don't touch the chicken yet.

02

Preheat the pan first

Largest skillet or wok, high heat, full 2 minutes before anything goes in. A drop of water should evaporate on contact. A warm pan means steaming regardless of everything else you do right.

03

Dry and coat right before cooking

Pat chicken strips completely dry. Toss with half the olive oil and the full spice blend just before it goes in the pan — not minutes earlier. Spices sitting on wet protein turn acrid at high heat.

04

Sear chicken — don't stir

Single layer, undisturbed 2–3 min. A crust needs to form while it sits still. Toss once, cook another 1–2 min. Remove and set aside.

05

Cook vegetables separately

Toss peppers and onions in remaining olive oil and a pinch of salt. Same hot pan, 3–4 min, stirring occasionally, until softened with some char. Adding them with the chicken drops the pan temperature and causes steaming.

06

Combine off the heat

Return chicken to the pan, pull off the burner, squeeze lime juice over everything. Off-heat acid stays bright — on-heat acid goes flat. Scatter coriander and serve immediately.

Optional

Char tortillas in the same dry pan while the chicken rests. Swap 2–3g chili powder for chipotle powder for smokier heat.
